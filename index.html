About

HexArctic helps business and communities grow even beyond the polar circle. Our tool analyses many data from orbital songs and land measurements units to find the best suitable spot for renewable energy production. Whether you want to build a data storage in cold arctic lands or find the best place for temporar station or habitat, we’ve got your energy needs covered.

Renewable Enegry in Arctic

Energy costs in the Arctic and sub-Arctic are among the highest in the world. Remote communities in the Arctic often run on diesel-powered microgrids, not connected to larger electricity grids.

Wind and solar energy sources are entirely dependent on weather, therefore mastering the ability to forecast the weather and hence the energy produced by wind and solar energy sources is the most important task that we are facing today.

Hydropower

The periodic annual meltdown of Arctic ices provides a predictable and stable renewable power source. TRhis is one of the reasons why hydropower in Arctic regions accounts for 40% of electricity generation.

Wind Power

Cold Arctic air is beneficial to wind energy production. Cold air is generally more dense than warm air, so at the same velocities, cold Arctic air increases the output energy productied by wind turbines.

Solar Power

The production of power in the Arctic is seasonal, since half of the year there is a lack of sunlight. Anyhow, sun reflecting from snow and low temperatures increase the output electricity of solar power cells, making it a great supplementary power source to other renewable energy production.
